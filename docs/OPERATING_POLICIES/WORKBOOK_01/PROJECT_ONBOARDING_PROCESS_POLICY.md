1.1 Inquiry Receipt & Logging
1.1 INQUIRY RECEIPT & LOGGING
1.1.1 Form Submission Protocol
Form Submission Protocol

All client inquiries must be submitted through official company channels (web form or designated email).
Every submission must include client name, contact information, and project description before it is processed.
Automated notifications generated by the inquiry system must be logged immediately in the project request portal.
Submissions containing unreadable, incomplete, or suspicious content must be flagged for manual review.
System-generated inquiry notifications must not be deleted before logging into the tracking system.

1.1.2 Project Request Handling Policy
Project Request Handling Policy

Every incoming inquiry must undergo a relevance and spam screening process.
Requests that fall outside the company's engineering services must be formally declined with a standard response.
Valid project requests must be converted into a project record within the project tracking system.
Client details must be entered exactly as provided without modification.
All request records must contain a timestamp and responsible onboarding officer.

1.1.3 Data Entry Protocol
Data Entry Protocol
All client and project information must be entered exactly as received without alteration.
Phone numbers, emails, and company names must be verified before submission into the system.
Data entry errors must be corrected immediately after detection and recorded in the audit trail.
No project record may remain incomplete within the project tracking system.
Administrative staff must ensure data consistency across CRM and project management systems.

1.1.4 Client Communication Policy
Client Communication Policy

All client communication must use approved email templates and official company accounts.
Client acknowledgment must be sent within 24 hours of receiving an inquiry.
Communication must be clear, professional, and technically accurate.
Any technical clarification provided to the client must be assisted by a technical support provider when necessary.
All client communications must be logged in the CRM system for traceability.

1.2 Initial Needs Assessment
1.2 INITIAL NEEDS ASSESSMENT
1.2.1-2Meeting Scheduling Policy
Meeting Scheduling Policy
Discovery meetings must be scheduled within the department's standard operating hours.
All meetings must include a formal agenda before the meeting occurs.
Calendar invitations must include a meeting link, agenda, and expected participants.
Meeting schedules must consider time zone differences when dealing with international clients.
Meeting confirmations must be obtained before the scheduled meeting time.

1.2.3 Meeting Facilitation Process
Meeting Facilitation Process
Meetings must focus on technical requirements, constraints, and expected outcomes.
Meeting facilitators must ensure all key project constraints are clearly discussed.
All meetings must produce documented notes or recordings.
Meeting participants must be given equal opportunity to clarify technical details.
Meeting facilitators must ensure scope assumptions are not left ambiguous.
1.2.4&1.4.3 Documentation Standards
Documentation Standards

All meeting notes must be converted into structured documentation after the meeting.
Technical terminology must be accurately transcribed without interpretation errors.
All documentation must be stored in the central documentation repository.
Documents must follow approved naming and versioning conventions.
Finalized documents may be distributed for internal review when necessary.

1.3 Project Feasibility Assessment
1.3 PROJECT FEASIBILITY ASSESSMENT
1.3.1 Internal Communication Protocol
Internal Communication Protocol

All feasibility review requests must be submitted through official internal communication systems.
Engineering teams must acknowledge receipt of review requests within one working day.
Review requests must include complete technical documentation before evaluation begins.
Review deadlines must be clearly specified in every request.

1.3.2 Strategic Performance Protocols
Strategic Performance Protocols

Engineering feasibility assessments must evaluate technical capability, available tools, and personnel expertise.
Projects exceeding internal capability must be declined or outsourced through formal approval.
Capability evaluations must consider project complexity and current operational workload.
All feasibility decisions and conclusions  must be documented  by the review team for management accountability.
Feasibility assessments must be recorded for performance tracking and future reference.

1.3.3 Risk & Safety Policy
Risk & Safety Policy
All projects must undergo formal engineering risk assessment during onboarding.
Safety hazards associated with the proposed project must be identified and documented.
Projects that present unacceptable safety risks must be rejected or redesigned before acceptance.
Risk mitigation strategies must be defined for identified engineering risks.
Risk evaluation must consider regulatory compliance requirements.
Risk reports must be reviewed by the Engineering Risk Officer before final approval of the project or commencement of fabrication.
Risk assessment documentation must be stored within the project records.

1.3.4 Review and Approval Policy
Review and Approval Policy

Engineering feasibility evaluations must be reviewed by qualified technical personnel before project acceptance decisions are made.
Risk and safety assessments must be reviewed to confirm that potential hazards and compliance requirements have been properly addressed.
Review comments and approval decisions must be documented and stored in the project documentation system.
Any issues identified during review must be resolved and documented before final approval is granted.
1.4 Scope & Budget Generation
1.4 SCOPE & BUDGET GENERATION
1.4.1 Scope Definition Policy
Scope Definition Policy

All engineering deliverables must be clearly defined in the Scope of Work.
Scope definitions must specify technical outputs, performance expectations, and deliverable formats.
Scope definitions must eliminate ambiguous or open-ended deliverables.
Scope documents must be approved by the Project Manager before contract drafting.
Any modification to scope must follow formal change management procedures.
Approved scope documents must be stored in the project documentation system.
Scope documentation must identify project constraints including cost, time, and resources.

1.4.2 Financial Control Policy
Financial Control Policy

All project cost estimates must follow approved financial estimation guidelines.

Budget projections must include engineering labor, operational costs, and material estimates.

Cost estimates must be reviewed by the finance team department before approval.

Budget assumptions must be documented clearly in the cost estimation report.

Budget estimates must align with company pricing strategies and profit targets.

Financial risks must be identified during the cost estimation process.

Approved cost estimates must be linked to the project onboarding record.
Financial terms must clearly define payment milestones, amount and conditions.

1.4.3 Documentation Standards
Documentation Standards

All meeting notes must be converted into structured documentation after the meeting.
Technical terminology must be accurately transcribed without interpretation errors.
All documentation must be stored in the central documentation repository.
Documents must follow approved naming and versioning conventions.
Finalized documents may be distributed for internal review when necessary.

1.5 Internal Onboarding Handoff
1.5 INTERNAL ONBOARDING HANDOFF
1.5.1 Internal Handoff Protocol
Internal Handoff Protocol

Completed onboarding documentation must be formally transferred to the contract administration team.

Project handoff for onboarding must occur only after scope, feasibility, and cost estimates are finalized.

The receiving department must acknowledge receipt of the project documentation.

Any missing documentation must be resolved before the handoff is completed.

Handoff meetings must ensure clear understanding of project scope and client requirements.

Handoff meetings must produce documented minutes.
Handoff communication must be conducted through approved internal communication systems (ERP, project management tools, or official email).
1.5.2 Project Portfolio Governance System
Project Portfolio Governance System

Project status changes must be updated in the project tracking system immediately.
All departments must rely on the central project dashboard for project status.
Status changes must be verified across all integrated systems.
No project may move to the next phase without system status confirmation.
Project tracking data must be maintained accurately at all times.
Unauthorized changes to project records are strictly prohibited.
The project portfolio system must maintain real-time visibility of active inquiries and onboarding projects.
Portfolio records must support management reporting and strategic oversight.

1.5.3 Document Repository Policy
Document Repository Policy

All onboarding documentation must be stored within the official project archive.

Archived records must follow approved naming and classification standards.
Access to archived files must follow information security guidelines.
Archived documentation must remain readable and accessible for audit purposes.
Backup copies of archived data must be maintained to prevent data loss.
