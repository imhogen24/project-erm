2.1 Contract Drafting
2.1 CONTRACT DRAFTING
2.1.1 & 2.1.3 Contract Drafting Policy
# Contract Drafting Policy
All contracts must be drafted using official company templates.
Contract content must strictly reflect the approved Scope of Work, deliverables, and cost estimates from onboarding.
All contractual terms must be clear, unambiguous, and legally enforceable.
Legal names, addresses, and financial values must be verified before draft completion.
Draft contracts must be stored in the contract management system with version control enabled.
Any deviation from standard templates must be justified and approved before inclusion.

2.1.2 Document Version Control Policy
# Document Version Control Policy
All contract documents must carry unique version numbers and revision dates.
Only the latest approved version of any contract may be used for review or execution.
Previous versions must be archived and protected from unauthorized modification.
Changes between versions must be clearly documented and traceable.
Unauthorized modification of approved templates is strictly prohibited.
Version control must be maintained within the centralized document management system.

2.2 Internal Review & Approval
2.2 INTERNAL REVIEW & APPROVAL
2.2.1 Internal Review Protocol
# Internal Review Protocol
All contracts must undergo mandatory internal review before submission to the client.
Technical terms must be reviewed by the Project Manager or Lead Engineer.
Financial terms must be reviewed and approved by the finance department.
Legal and compliance aspects must be reviewed by authorized personnel.
All review responses must be documented and resolved before approval.
Contracts must not proceed to the client without formal internal approval confirmation.
Review timelines must be adhered to in order to avoid project delays.
All review activities must be logged in the internal project review system.
Incomplete review requests must be returned to the requesting department.

2.2.2 Financial Control Policy
Financial Control Policy

All project cost estimates must follow approved financial estimation guidelines.

Budget projections must include engineering labor, operational costs, and material estimates.

Cost estimates must be reviewed by the finance team department before approval.

Budget assumptions must be documented clearly in the cost estimation report.

Budget estimates must align with company pricing strategies and profit targets.

Financial risks must be identified during the cost estimation process.

Approved cost estimates must be linked to the project onboarding record.
Financial terms must clearly define payment milestones, amount and conditions.

2.2.3 Corporate Governance Policy
# Corporate Governance Policy
All contracts must comply with company legal standards and regulatory requirements.
Liability exposure must be evaluated before contract approval.
Intellectual property clauses must protect company engineering outputs.
Contracts must not expose the company to unacceptable financial or legal risk.
Only authorized personnel may approve contracts based on governance hierarchy.
Any governance breach must be reported to senior management immediately.
Contractual obligations must align with approved business objectives and risk tolerance.

2.3 Client Negotiation
2.3 CLIENT NEGOTIATION
2.3.1 Client Communication Policy
Client Communication Policy
All client communication must use approved email templates and official company accounts.
Client acknowledgment must be sent within 24 hours of receiving an inquiry.
Communication must be clear, professional, and technically accurate.
Any technical clarification provided to the client must be assisted by a technical support provider when necessary.
All client communications must be logged in the CRM system for traceability.

2.3.2 Meeting Scheduling Policy
Meeting Scheduling Policy
Discovery meetings must be scheduled within the department's standard operating hours.
All meetings must include a formal agenda before the meeting occurs.
Calendar invitations must include a meeting link, agenda, and expected participants.
Meeting schedules must consider time zone differences when dealing with international clients.
Meeting confirmations must be obtained before the scheduled meeting time.

2.3.3 Client Engagement Policy
# Client Engagement Policy
All contract-related communication must be conducted through official company communication channels.
Clients must be provided with clear explanations of contract and project terms when required.
All negotiations and discussions must be documented and stored in the contract records.
Response timelines to client inquiries must be maintained to ensure efficiency.
Technical clarifications must involve relevant engineering personnel when necessary.
Commitments made during negotiations must not contradict approved project scope or policies.

2.3.4 Contract Revision Policy
# Contract Revision Policy
All client-requested changes must be formally documented before implementation.
All revised clauses must undergo internal review before acceptance.
Contract negotiations must maintain transparent revision records.
Major revisions affecting scope, cost, or timeline must require management reapproval.
Informal or verbal contract modifications are not permitted.
Final revised contracts must be clearly marked as approved versions.

2.4 Execution & Archiving
2.4 EXECUTION & ARCHIVING
2.4.1 & 2.4.3 Document Execution Policy
# Document Execution Policy
All contracts must be fully executed (signed by both parties and timestamped) before project initiation.
Executed contracts and documents must include complete and verifiable signature records.
Final signed contracts and documents must be stored in the central contract repository.
Clients must receive final signed copies immediately after execution.
Execution status must be updated in the project management system.
Contract execution must trigger activation of project financial and operational workflows.
No project activity may commence without confirmed contract execution.

2.4.2 Signature Authority Policy
# Signature Authority Policy
Only designated personnel with formal authorization may sign contracts on behalf of the company.
Signature authority must follow the defined organizational hierarchy.
Contracts signed without proper authorization are invalid and non-binding.
Digital signatures must be executed through approved secure platforms.
Signed contracts must include verifiable audit trails.
Signature records must include date, time, and identity verification.

2.4.4 Data Retention Policy
# Data Retention Policy
All documents must be archived in secure digital repositories.
Contracts, documents and engineering data must be retained according to organizational and legal retention requirements.
Archived documents must remain accessible for audits, future reference and legal reference.
Records must be protected against unauthorized access or loss.
Deletion or disposal of records must require formal authorization.
Backup systems must ensure redundancy of data.

2.4.5 Project Portfolio Governance System
Project Portfolio Governance System

Project status changes must be updated in the project tracking system immediately.
All departments must rely on the central project dashboard for project status.
Status changes must be verified across all integrated systems.
No project may move to the next phase without system status confirmation.
Project tracking data must be maintained accurately at all times.
Unauthorized changes to project records are strictly prohibited.
The project portfolio system must maintain real-time visibility of active inquiries and onboarding projects.
Portfolio records must support management reporting and strategic oversight.
