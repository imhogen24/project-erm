# 3.1 Payment Schedule Setup
# 3.1 PAYMENT SCHEDULING SETUP
# 3.1.1 Inter-departmental Handoff Policy
# Inter-Departmental Handoff Policy
All inter-departmental handoffs must be formal, documented, and executed through approved internal systems.

A handoff may only occur when all required inputs, approvals, and documentation for the receiving department are complete.

The receiving department must acknowledge receipt of the handoff before assuming responsibility.

Any missing documentation or unclear information must be resolved before the handoff is completed.

Handoff meetings must ensure clear understanding of project scope and client requirements.

Handoffs involving financial dependencies must ensure that payment status and financial approvals are clearly communicated.
Handoff communication must be conducted through approved internal communication systems (ERP, project management tools, or official email).
Inter-departmental dependencies must be tracked within the central project or workflow management system.
Handoff record must include; Date & Time, Sending Department, Receiving Department, Responsible Personnel and Status of the Handoff.
Standardized handoff templates must be used to ensure consistency across all departments.
# 3.1.2 Billing & Scheduling Policy
# Billing & Scheduling Policy
A project-specific payment schedule must be established immediately after contract execution.
Payment schedules must align with contractual milestones and deliverables.
Payment timelines must be clearly communicated to the client and internal teams.
Any modification to payment schedules must follow formal approval procedures.
Payment schedules must be logged and tracked within the financial system.
Internal teams must be notified of payment dependencies before progressing to new project phases.
Projects must not proceed to critical stages without confirmation of required payments.

# 3.1.3 Data Entry Protocol
# Data Entry Protocol
All client and project information must be entered exactly as received without alteration.
Phone numbers, emails, and company names must be verified before submission into the system.
Data entry errors must be corrected immediately after detection and recorded in the audit trail.
No project record may remain incomplete within the project tracking system.
Administrative staff must ensure data consistency across CRM and project management systems.
# 3.2 Invoicing & Billing
# 3.2 INVOICING & BILLING
# 3.2.1 - 2 Invoicing Policy
# Invoicing Policy
All invoices must be generated from the approved financial management system.

Invoice values must correspond strictly to contract milestones or payment schedules.
Each invoice must include project reference, contract details, and payment terms.
Tax and statutory requirements must be accurately applied to all invoices.
All invoices must undergo internal verification before being sent to the client.
Invoices must be issued promptly upon milestone completion or as scheduled.
Copies of all invoices must be stored within the financial records system.

# 3.2.3 Financial Control Policy
# Financial Control Policy

All project cost estimates must follow approved financial estimation guidelines.

Budget projections must include engineering labor, operational costs, and material estimates.

Cost estimates must be reviewed by the finance team department before approval.

Budget assumptions must be documented clearly in the cost estimation report.

Budget estimates must align with company pricing strategies and profit targets.

Financial risks must be identified during the cost estimation process.

Approved cost estimates must be linked to the project onboarding record.
Financial terms must clearly define payment milestones, amount and conditions.
# 3.2.4 Client Communication Policy
# Client Communication Policy

All client communication must use approved email templates and official company accounts.
Client acknowledgment must be sent within 24 hours of receiving an inquiry.
Communication must be clear, professional, and technically accurate.
Any technical clarification provided to the client must be assisted by a technical support provider when necessary.
All client communications must be logged in the CRM system for traceability.
# 3.3 Reconciliation & Triggering
# 3.3 RECONCILATION & TRIGGERING
# 3.3.1 Treasury Management Policy
# Treasury Management Policy
All project revenues must be deposited into the designated corporate accounts.
Project funds must be tracked and allocated per project within the financial management system.
Daily monitoring of bank balances and transactions must be conducted by the finance team.
All cash inflows and outflows must be recorded in real time within the accounting system.
Unauthorized withdrawals, transfers, or use of company funds are strictly prohibited.
Treasury operations must ensure sufficient liquidity to support ongoing project activities.

# 3.3.2 Financial Reconciliation Policy
# Financial Reconciliation Policy
Incoming payments must be reconciled against issued invoices within 24 hours of receipt.

Financial records must match bank statements and accounting entries.

Discrepancies must be reported immediately to the finance supervisor.

Monthly reconciliation reports must be submitted to management for review.

All reconciliation activities must be documented for audit purposes.
Reconciliation processes must ensure accuracy of financial reporting and records.

# 3.3.3 Documentation Policy
# Documentation Standards

All meeting notes must be converted into structured documentation after the meeting.
Technical terminology must be accurately transcribed without interpretation errors.
All documentation must be stored in the central documentation repository.
Documents must follow approved naming and versioning conventions.
Finalized documents may be distributed for internal review when necessary.

# 3.3.4 Project Portfolio Governance System
# Project Portfolio Governance System

Project status changes must be updated in the project tracking system immediately.
All departments must rely on the central project dashboard for project status.
Status changes must be verified across all integrated systems.
No project may move to the next phase without system status confirmation.
Project tracking data must be maintained accurately at all times.
Unauthorized changes to project records are strictly prohibited.
The project portfolio system must maintain real-time visibility of active inquiries and onboarding projects.
Portfolio records must support management reporting and strategic oversight.

# 3.4 Outstanding Payment Management
# 3.4 OUTSTANDING PAYMENT MANAGEMENT
# 3.4.1 Credit Control Policy
# Credit Control Policy
All outstanding invoices must be continuously monitored by the finance department.
Clients must receive automated and manual reminders prior to payment due dates.
Overdue payments must trigger structured follow-up procedures and escalation.
Credit terms must be defined clearly in the contract and strictly enforced.
Extension of payment deadlines must require formal management approval.
Projects may be suspended if payment delays exceed contractual thresholds.

# 3.4.2 Outstanding Collections Policy
# Outstanding Collections Policy
All incoming payments must be monitored and confirmed by the finance team.
Payment receipts must be issued to clients within one working day of confirmation.
Payments must be matched against corresponding invoices immediately upon receipt.
Partial payments must be clearly recorded and tracked against outstanding balances.
Unidentified payments must be investigated and resolved promptly.
Payment confirmations must be updated in the project and financial systems.
Clients must be notified in cases of payment discrepancies or shortfalls.

# 3.4.3 Risk & Financial Liability Policy
# Risk & Financial Liability Policy
All projects must undergo financial risk assessment during payment planning.
Risks related to delayed payments must be identified and mitigated early.
High-value projects must include risk buffers or contingency provisions.
Financial risks must be reviewed periodically throughout the project lifecycle.
Risk mitigation strategies must be documented and approved by management.
Financial risks impacting project execution must be escalated immediately.
