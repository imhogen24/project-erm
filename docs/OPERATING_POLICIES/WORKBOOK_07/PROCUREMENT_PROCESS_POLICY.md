# 7.1 BOM Handover & Review
# 7.1 BOM HANDOVER & REVIEW
# 7.1.1 Inter-departmental Handoff Policy
# Inter-Departmental Handoff Policy
All inter-departmental handoffs must be formal, documented, and executed through approved internal systems.

A handoff may only occur when all required inputs, approvals, and documentation for the receiving department are complete.

The receiving department must acknowledge receipt of the handoff before assuming responsibility.

Any missing documentation or unclear information must be resolved before the handoff is completed.

Handoff meetings must ensure clear understanding of project scope and client requirements.

Handoffs involving financial dependencies must ensure that payment status and financial approvals are clearly communicated.
Handoff communication must be conducted through approved internal communication systems (ERP, project management tools, or official email).
Inter-departmental dependencies must be tracked within the central project or workflow management system.
Handoff record must include; Date & Time, Sending Department, Receiving Department, Responsible Personnel and Status of the Handoff.
Standardized handoff templates must be used to ensure consistency across all departments.

# 7.1.2 Documentation Standards
# Documentation Standards
All meeting notes must be converted into structured documentation after the meeting.
Technical terminology must be accurately transcribed without interpretation errors.
All documentation must be stored in the central documentation repository.
Documents must follow approved naming and versioning conventions.
Finalized documents may be distributed for internal review when necessary.

# 7.2 Budget Validation & Sourcing
# 7.2 BUDGET VALIDATION & SOURCING
# 7.2.1 Financial Control Policy
# Financial Control Policy

All project cost estimates must follow approved financial estimation guidelines.

Budget projections must include engineering labor, operational costs, and material estimates.

Cost estimates must be reviewed by the finance team department before approval.

Budget assumptions must be documented clearly in the cost estimation report.

Budget estimates must align with company pricing strategies and profit targets.

Financial risks must be identified during the cost estimation process.

Approved cost estimates must be linked to the project onboarding record.
Financial terms must clearly define payment milestones, amount and conditions.

# 7.2.2 & 7.2.4 Vendor Sourcing Policy
# Vendor Sourcing Policy

Vendors must be sourced based on their ability to meet technical specifications, quality standards, and delivery timelines.
Supplier performance must be reviewed periodically and documented.
Procurement teams must maintain a list of approved vendors.
Vendor relationships must comply with ethical sourcing standards.
Vendor sourcing decisions must be based on documented evaluation criteria.
New vendors must undergo initial assessment before engagement.
Vendor capabilities must align with project-specific material and service requirements.
Vendor sourcing activities must be documented for traceability and accountability.
# 7.2.3 Vendor Communication Protocol
# Vendor Communication Protocol
All vendor communication must be conducted through official company communication channels.
Procurement requirements must be communicated to vendors with clear technical specifications, quantities, and delivery expectations.
Clarifications from vendors must be reviewed and validated before decisions are made.
Changes in requirements must be formally communicated and acknowledged by vendors.
Communication records must be maintained within the procurement documentation system.
Informal or undocumented agreements with vendors are strictly prohibited.

# 7.3  PURCHASING & PO ISSUANCE

## Purchasing Policy

Procurement activities must comply with approved budgets and financial controls.
All purchasing activities must be documented and traceable within the procurement system.
Changes to purchase orders must be formally reviewed and approved.
Procurement decisions must consider cost, quality, and delivery performance.

# 7.4 Material Receiving & QA
# 7.4 MATERIAL RECEIVING & QA
# 7.4.1 Receiving Protocol
# Receiving Protocol
All incoming materials must undergo formal receiving and inspection before acceptance.
Received items must be verified against; purchase order, delivery notes and technical specification.
Any damaged, defective, or non-conforming materials must be rejected or flagged for review.
Acceptance of materials must be documented with receiving records and responsible personnel identification.
Materials must not be released for use until receiving verification is completed.
Receiving activities must be logged in the inventory or procurement system.
Supporting documents (delivery notes, invoices) must be attached to receiving records.

# 7.4.2 Quality Assurance Guidelines
# Quality Assurance (QA) Guidelines
All deliverables must undergo quality assurance checks before submission to the client or fabrication process.
QA activities must verify accuracy, completeness, and compliance with design specifications.
QA checks must ensure alignment with approved requirements and engineering standards.
QA results must be documented and attached to the deliverable records.
QA responsibilities must be assigned to qualified personnel independent of the design creator where applicable.
No deliverable may be shared with the client without QA clearance in a review and approval situation.

# 7.4.3 Inventory Management Policy
# Inventory Management Policy

All materials must be recorded in the inventory system upon arrival.
Inventory must be tracked based on project allocation and usage.
Storage areas must follow material handling safety standards.
Inventory discrepancies must be identified,  investigated and resolved  immediately.
Periodic inventory audits must be conducted to ensure accuracy and accountability.
Access to inventory must be restricted to authorized personnel.

# 7.4.4 Project Portfolio Governance System
# Project Portfolio Governance System

Project status changes must be updated in the project tracking system immediately.
All departments must rely on the central project dashboard for project status.
Status changes must be verified across all integrated systems.
No project may move to the next phase without system status confirmation.
Project tracking data must be maintained accurately at all times.
Unauthorized changes to project records are strictly prohibited.
The project portfolio system must maintain real-time visibility of active inquiries and onboarding projects.
Portfolio records must support management reporting and strategic oversight.
