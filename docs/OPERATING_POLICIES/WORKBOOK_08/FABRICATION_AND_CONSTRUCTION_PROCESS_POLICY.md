# 8.1 Fabrication Planning
# 8.1 FABRICATION PLANNING
# 8.1.1 Inter-departmental Handoff Policy
# Inter-Departmental Handoff Policy
All inter-departmental handoffs must be formal, documented, and executed through approved internal systems.

A handoff may only occur when all required inputs, approvals, and documentation for the receiving department are complete.

The receiving department must acknowledge receipt of the handoff before assuming responsibility.

Any missing documentation or unclear information must be resolved before the handoff is completed.

Handoff meetings must ensure clear understanding of project scope and client requirements.

Handoffs involving financial dependencies must ensure that payment status and financial approvals are clearly communicated.
Handoff communication must be conducted through approved internal communication systems (ERP, project management tools, or official email).
Inter-departmental dependencies must be tracked within the central project or workflow management system.
Handoff record must include; Date & Time, Sending Department, Receiving Department, Responsible Personnel and Status of the Handoff.
Standardized handoff templates must be used to ensure consistency across all departments.

# 8.1.2 Production Sequencing Policy
# Production Sequencing Policy
Fabrication activities must follow a defined and approved production sequence.
Sequence planning must consider process dependencies and workflow efficiency.
Activities must be executed in the correct order to prevent rework or process conflict.
Any deviation from the approved sequence must be reviewed and authorized.
Production sequencing must be documented and communicated to all relevant personnel.
Sequence changes must be tracked and controlled within the production system.
Sequencing must align with project timelines and delivery requirements.

# 8.1.3 Workshop Resource Policy
# Workshop Resource Policy
All workshop activities must utilize approved and functional equipment and tools.
Equipment must be available and inspected for operational readiness before use.
Only trained and authorized personnel may operate fabrication machinery.
Workshop resources must be allocated based on production requirements and schedules.
Tools and equipment must be used strictly according to operational guidelines.
Resource usage must be monitored to ensure efficiency and prevent misuse.
Any equipment malfunction must be reported and resolved before continued use.

# 8.2 Pre-Fabrication Briefing
# 8.2 PRE-FABRICATION BRIEFING
# 8.2.1 Occupational Health & Safety Policy
# Occupational Health & Safety Policy

All personnel must wear appropriate protective equipment (PPE).
Hazardous materials must be handled according to safety guidelines.
Hazardous operations must be conducted under controlled and supervised conditions.
All incidents, near-misses, and accidents must be reported and documented immediately.
Safety training must be mandatory for all personnel involved in fabrication and operational activities.
Work must be halted if unsafe conditions are identified.

# 8.2.2 Risk & Safety Policy
# Risk & Safety Policy
All projects must undergo formal engineering risk assessment during onboarding.
Safety hazards associated with the proposed project must be identified and documented.
Projects that present unacceptable safety risks must be rejected or redesigned before acceptance.
Risk mitigation strategies must be defined for identified engineering risks.
Risk evaluation must consider regulatory compliance requirements.
Risk reports must be reviewed by the Engineering Risk Officer before final approval of the project or commencement of fabrication .
Risk assessment documentation must be stored within the project records.

# 8.3.1 Material Handling Policy
# Material Handling Policy
All materials must be handled in a manner that prevents damage, contamination, or deformation.
Lifting and handling equipment must be used in accordance with load capacity and safety guidelines.
Personnel must follow approved handling procedures for different material types.
Sensitive or precision components must receive special handling protection.
Storage and movement of materials must ensure safety and accessibility.
Handling incidents must be reported and documented.
Materials must be properly labeled and tracked during handling operations.

# 8.3.2 Manufacturing Standards
# Manufacturing Standards Policy
All fabrication activities must follow approved engineering drawings and specifications.
Manufacturing processes must comply with established engineering and industry standards.

# 8.4 Active Fabrication & Assembly
# 8.4 ACTIVE FABRICATION & ASSEMBLY
# 8.4.1 Assembly Modeling Protocol
# Assembly Modelling Protocol

All prepared materials must be loaded into designated fixtures, jigs, or machine beds in accordance with defined setup procedures.
Only approved fixtures and jigs may be used to ensure consistency in alignment and positioning.
Materials must be properly seated and stabilized before any clamping or fastening is applied.
All locating pins must be fully engaged and correctly positioned to establish accurate reference points.
Clamps and holding mechanisms must be securely fastened to prevent movement during operation.
No machining or fabrication process may begin unless all locating pins and clamps are fully seated and verified.
Any misalignment, improper seating, or instability must result in immediate correction before proceeding.
Assembly/setup activities must be carried out by trained and authorized personnel only.

# 8.4.3 Process Distortion &Tolerance Control Policy
# Process Distortion & Tolerance Control Policy
