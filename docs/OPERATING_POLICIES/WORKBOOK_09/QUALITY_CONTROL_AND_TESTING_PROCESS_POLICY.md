# 9.1 INSPECTION PREPARATION
# 9.1.1 Inter-departmental Handoff Policy
# Inter-Departmental Handoff Policy
All inter-departmental handoffs must be formal, documented, and executed through approved internal systems.

A handoff may only occur when all required inputs, approvals, and documentation for the receiving department are complete.

The receiving department must acknowledge receipt of the handoff before assuming responsibility.

Any missing documentation or unclear information must be resolved before the handoff is completed.

Handoff meetings must ensure clear understanding of project scope and client requirements.

Handoffs involving financial dependencies must ensure that payment status and financial approvals are clearly communicated.
Handoff communication must be conducted through approved internal communication systems (ERP, project management tools, or official email).
Inter-departmental dependencies must be tracked within the central project or workflow management system.
Handoff record must include; Date & Time, Sending Department, Receiving Department, Responsible Personnel and Status of the Handoff.
Standardized handoff templates must be used to ensure consistency across all departments.

# 9.1.2 Documentation Standards
# Documentation Standards
All meeting notes must be converted into structured documentation after the meeting.
Technical terminology must be accurately transcribed without interpretation errors.
All documentation must be stored in the central documentation repository.
Documents must follow approved naming and versioning conventions.
Finalized documents may be distributed for internal review when necessary.

# 9.2 Visual & Surface Inspection
# 9.2 VISUAL & SURFACE INSPECTION
# 9.2.1 Surface Finish Standards
# Surface Finish Standards
Surface finishes must meet specified engineering and functional requirements.
Finishing processes must be controlled to ensure consistency and quality.
Surface defects must be identified and corrected before final acceptance.
Finishing methods must align with material and application requirements.
Surface finish measurements must be documented where applicable.
Surface quality must be maintained throughout handling and storage.

# 9.2 DIMENSIONAL & GD&T INSPECTION
# 9.2.2 Tolerance & Capability Policy
# Tolerance and Capability Policy
All engineering designs must define tolerances based on functional requirements and performance expectations.
Tolerance values must be aligned with manufacturing and fabrication capabilities.
Capability assessments must be conducted to ensure design feasibility within available production processes.
Overly tight or unrealistic tolerances must be avoided unless technically justified.
Tolerance specifications must be clearly documented in all relevant engineering drawings.
Capability limitations must be identified and communicated during the design process.
Tolerance and capability decisions must be reviewed and approved before design release.

# 9.3 Functional & Load Setup
# 9.3 FUNCTIONAL & LOAD SETUP
# 9.3.1 Risk & Safety Policy
# Risk & Safety Policy
All projects must undergo formal engineering risk assessment during onboarding.
Safety hazards associated with the proposed project must be identified and documented.
Projects that present unacceptable safety risks must be rejected or redesigned before acceptance.
Risk mitigation strategies must be defined for identified engineering risks.
Risk evaluation must consider regulatory compliance requirements.
Risk reports must be reviewed by the Engineering Risk Officer before final approval of the project or commencement of fabrication .
Risk assessment documentation must be stored within the project records.
# 9.3 Functional & Load Execution
# 9.3 FUNCTIONAL & LOAD EXECUTION
# 9.3.2 Design Validation Policy
# Design Validation Policy

All completed designs must undergo formal validation against approved design specifications and requirements.
Validation methods must include appropriate testing, simulation, or analytical verification where applicable.
Testing methods must follow recognized engineering standards.
Any discrepancies between design outputs and requirements must be identified, documented, and resolved.
Validation results must be reviewed and approved by authorized technical personnel.
No design may proceed to final production or delivery without successful validation confirmation.
Validation activities must maintain traceability to original design requirements.
Changes made after validation must trigger re-validation where necessary.
All validation records must be documented and stored for audit and reference purposes.
# 9.4 Non-Conformance Handling
# 9.4 NON-CONFORMANCE HANDLING
# 9.4.1 In-Process Quality Control Policy
# In-Process Quality Control Policy
Quality checks must be conducted at defined stages during the fabrication and testing process.
Inspection criteria must align with engineering specifications and standards.
Non-conforming work must be identified and corrected immediately.
Inspection results must be recorded and maintained.
Quality control activities must be performed by qualified personnel.
Work must not proceed to the next stage without meeting quality requirements.
In-process quality data must support continuous improvement and traceability.
# 9.4.2 Change Management Policy
# Change Management Policy
All client change requests must be formally documented before implementation.
Changes affecting scope, cost, or timeline must trigger project reassessment.
No changes may be implemented without formal approval from authorized personnel.
Approved changes must be communicated to all affected departments.
Change requests must be tracked within the project management system.
Change impact assessments must be documented and reviewed before approval
Change history must be maintained for project accountability.

# 9.5 Final Validation & Release
# 9.5 FINAL VALIDATION & RELEASE
# 9.5.1 Final Testing & Inspection Policy
# Final Testing & Inspection Policy
All fabricated or constructed outputs must undergo final testing and inspection before delivery or handoff.
Final inspection must verify compliance with; engineering drawings, specifications, and quality standards.
Inspection must confirm that all components are complete, functional, and free from defects.
Any non-conformance identified during final inspection must be corrected before approval.
Final inspection and testing must be conducted by qualified and authorized personnel.
Inspection and test results must be documented and recorded.
No product or system may be released without formal approval after final inspection.
Final inspection records must be retained for traceability, compliance, and audit purposes.

# 9.5.2 Project Portfolio Governance System
# Project Portfolio Governance System

Project status changes must be updated in the project tracking system immediately.
All departments must rely on the central project dashboard for project status.
Status changes must be verified across all integrated systems.
No project may move to the next phase without system status confirmation.
Project tracking data must be maintained accurately at all times.
Unauthorized changes to project records are strictly prohibited.
The project portfolio system must maintain real-time visibility of active inquiries and onboarding projects.
Portfolio records must support management reporting and strategic oversight.
