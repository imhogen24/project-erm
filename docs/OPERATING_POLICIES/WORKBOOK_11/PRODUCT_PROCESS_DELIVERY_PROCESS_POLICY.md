# 11.1 Logistics & Dispatch Planning
# 11.1 LOGISTICS & DISPATCHING PLANNING
# 11.1.1 Client Engagement Policy
# Client Engagement Policy
All contract-related communication must be conducted through official company communication channels.
Clients must be provided with clear explanations of contract, deliveries and project terms when required.
All negotiations and discussions must be documented and stored in the contract records.
Response timelines to client inquiries must be maintained to ensure efficiency.
Technical clarifications must involve relevant engineering personnel when necessary.
Commitments made during negotiations must not contradict approved project scope or policies.

# 11.1.2 Distribution Properties Policy
# Distribution Properties Policy

Products must be delivered using appropriate transport arrangements (e.g; vehicles).
Delivery logistics must consider product dimensions and weight.
Delivery schedules must be confirmed with clients in advance.
Shipping documentation must accompany all delivered products.
Delivery records must be maintained for accountability.

# 11.2 Packaging & Load Securing
# 11.2 PACKAGING & LOAD SECURING
# 11.2.1 Material Handling Policy
# Material Handling Policy
All materials must be handled in a manner that prevents damage, contamination, or deformation.
Lifting and handling equipment must be used in accordance with load capacity and safety guidelines.
Personnel must follow approved handling procedures for different material types.
Sensitive or precision components must receive special handling protection.
Storage and movement of materials must ensure safety and accessibility.
Handling incidents must be reported and documented.
Materials must be properly labeled and tracked during handling operations.

# 11.2.2 Documentation Delivery Policy
# Documentation Delivery Policy

All engineering documentation must be complete, accurate, organized and aligned with the final approved project outputs.
Documentation delivery must be executed through approved and secure delivery channels.
A formal record of documentation delivery must be maintained, including; Date of Delivery, Recipient Details, Delivered Document List, and Responsible Personnel.
Recipients must acknowledge receipt of documentation where required.
Any post-delivery corrections or updates must follow revision control and re-delivery procedures.
Documentation must be delivered in formats that are accessible, usable, and compliant with project requirements.
Incomplete or unapproved documentation must not be released under any circumstances.
Documentation delivery must ensure traceability between final outputs and archived records.

# 11.3 On-Site Unloading & Setup
# 11.3 ON-SITE UNLOADING & SETUP
# 11.3.1 Liquidation Properties Policy
# Liquidation Properties Policy

All delivered equipment or products must undergo controlled and supervised unloading at the designated site.
Unloading activities must be planned to ensure safety, equipment integrity, and environmental suitability.
Appropriate handling equipment (e.g; cranes, forklifts, lifting gear) must be used according to load requirements and safety standards.
Only trained and authorized personnel may supervise and execute unloading operations.
All items must be moved and positioned within the designated operational footprint as defined in the project layout.
Temporary placement outside the designated footprint is not permitted unless formally approved.
Client or site representative presence must be ensured during unloading and placement where required.
Any incidents during unloading or placement must be reported and addressed immediately.
Equipment must not proceed to operational use until unloading and placement are verified and approved.

# 11.3.2 Quality Assurance Guidelines
# Quality Assurance (QA) Guidelines
All deliverables must undergo quality assurance checks before submission to the client or fabrication process.
QA activities must verify accuracy, completeness, and compliance with design specifications.
QA checks must ensure alignment with approved requirements and engineering standards.
QA results must be documented and attached to the deliverable records.
QA responsibilities must be assigned to qualified personnel independent of the design creator where applicable.
No deliverable may be shared with the client without QA clearance in a review and approval situation.

# 11.4 Site Acceptance Walkthrough
# 11.4 SITE ACCEPTANCE WALKTHROUGH
# 11.4.1 Client Training & Handover Policy
# Client Training & Handover Policy

All deliverables must be accompanied by structured client training and formal handover procedures.
Training must cover:
Operation of the product or system
Maintenance requirements
Safety procedures
Limitations and constraints
Training materials must be prepared, reviewed, and aligned with delivered systems or processes.
Handover must include; Technical documentation, User manuals and Operational guidelines.
Training sessions must be conducted by qualified personnel.
Client understanding must be verified through interaction, demonstration, or feedback during training.
Handover must be formally acknowledged by the client upon completion.
Training and handover activities must be recorded and stored for reference and accountability.

# 11.4 Formal Handover & Sign-Off
# 11.4 FORMAL HANDOVER & SIGN-OFF
# 11.4.2 Document Execution Policy
# Document Execution Policy
All contracts must be fully executed (signed by both parties and timestamped) before project initiation.
Executed contracts and documents must include complete and verifiable signature records.
Final signed contracts and documents must be stored in the central contract repository.
Clients must receive final signed copies immediately after execution.
Execution status must be updated in the project management system.
Contract execution must trigger activation of project financial and operational workflows.
No project activity may commence without confirmed contract execution.
# 11.5 Commercial Close-Out
# 11.5 COMMERCIAL CLOSE-OUT
# 11.5.1 Data Retention Policy
# Data Retention Policy
All documents must be archived in secure digital repositories.
Contracts, documents and engineering data must be retained according to organizational and legal retention requirements.
Archived documents must remain accessible for audits, future reference and legal reference.
Records must be protected against unauthorized access or loss.
Deletion or disposal of records must require formal authorization.
Backup systems must ensure redundancy of data.

# 11.5.2 Financial Control Policy
# Financial Control Policy

All project cost estimates must follow approved financial estimation guidelines.

Budget projections must include engineering labor, operational costs, and material estimates.

Cost estimates must be reviewed by the finance team department before approval.

Budget assumptions must be documented clearly in the cost estimation report.

Budget estimates must align with company pricing strategies and profit targets.

Financial risks must be identified during the cost estimation process.

Approved cost estimates must be linked to the project onboarding record.
Financial terms must clearly define payment milestones, amount and conditions.
