# 12.1.2 Training & Development Policy
# Training & Development Policy

All delivered products or systems must be supported with structured training programs tailored to the client’s operational needs.
Training must be aligned with; Delivered system functionality, Operational requirements and User roles & responsibilities.
Training content must cover; System operation, Maintenance procedures, Safety practices and Troubleshooting guidelines.
Training materials must be prepared, standardized, and validated before delivery to the client.
Training must be conducted using a combination of theoretical instruction and practical demonstration.
Training sessions must be delivered by qualified personnel with technical knowledge of the system.
Client personnel participation must be documented and tracked.
Any gaps in client understanding must be identified and addressed during training sessions.
Training records must be stored for reference, compliance, and future support activities.
Training programs must support long-term operational efficiency and correct system usage.

# 12.2 Training Execution
# 12.2 TRAINING EXECUTION
# 12.2.1 Occupational Health & Safety Policy
# Occupational Health & Safety Policy

All personnel must wear appropriate protective equipment (PPE).
Hazardous materials must be handled according to safety guidelines.
Hazardous operations must be conducted under controlled and supervised conditions.
All incidents, near-misses, and accidents must be reported and documented immediately.
Safety training must be mandatory for all personnel involved in fabrication and operational activities.
Work must be halted if unsafe conditions are identified.

# 12.2.2 Client Training & Handover Policy
# Client Training & Handover Policy

All deliverables must be accompanied by structured client training and formal handover procedures.
Training must cover:
Operation of the product or system
Maintenance requirements
Safety procedures
Limitations and constraints
Training materials must be prepared, reviewed, and aligned with delivered systems or processes.
Handover must include; Technical documentation, User manuals and Operational guidelines.
Training sessions must be conducted by qualified personnel.
Client understanding must be verified through interaction, demonstration, or feedback during training.
Handover must be formally acknowledged by the client upon completion.
Training and handover activities must be recorded and stored for reference and accountability.

# 12.3 Support Window Activation
# 12.3 SUPPORT WINDOW ACTIVATION
# 12.3.1 Post-Delivery Support Policy
# Post-Delivery Support Policy

Post-delivery support must be provided to ensure continued functionality and performance of delivered systems.
Support channels must be clearly defined and communicated to the client.
All client-reported issues must be formally logged and tracked within the support system.
Response and resolution timelines must be defined and adhered to.
Technical support must be provided by qualified personnel with knowledge of the delivered system.
Updates, fixes, or modifications must follow approved change and documentation procedures.
Client feedback during support interactions must be captured and used to improve future processes.
Support services must align with contractual agreements and service expectations.
Post-delivery support records must be retained for traceability and performance monitoring.
Recurring issues must trigger corrective and preventive actions.

# 12.3 Support Ticketing & Resolution
# 12.3 SUPPORT TICKETING AND RESOLUTION
# 12.3.2 Feedback Handling Procedure
# Feedback Handling Procedure

All client feedback must be captured and documented immediately.
Feedback must be categorized as clarification, revision, or change request.
Engineers must review feedback to determine technical feasibility and validity.
Feedback responses must be communicated back to the client clearly.
Feedback records must remain attached to the project documentation.
No feedback may be acted upon without formal documentation and internal acknowledgment.

# 12.4 Operational Audit
# 12.4 OPERATIONAL AUDIT
# 12.4.1 Quality Assurance Guidelines
# Quality Assurance (QA) Guidelines
All deliverables must undergo quality assurance checks before submission to the client or fabrication process.
QA activities must verify accuracy, completeness, and compliance with design specifications.
QA checks must ensure alignment with approved requirements and engineering standards.
QA results must be documented and attached to the deliverable records.
QA responsibilities must be assigned to qualified personnel independent of the design creator where applicable.
No deliverable may be shared with the client without QA clearance in a review and approval situation.

# 12.5 Project & Support Closure
# 12.5 PROJECT & SUPPORT CLOSURE
# 12.5.1 Corporate Governance Policy
# Corporate Governance Policy
All contracts must comply with company legal standards and regulatory requirements.
Liability exposure must be evaluated before contract approval.
Intellectual property clauses must protect company engineering outputs.
Contracts must not expose the company to unacceptable financial or legal risk.
Only authorized personnel may approve contracts based on governance hierarchy.
Any governance breach must be reported to senior management immediately.
Contractual obligations must align with approved business objectives and risk tolerance.

# 12.5 Final System Archival
# 12.5 FINAL SYSTEM ARCHIVAL
# 12.5.2 Data Retention Policy
# Data Retention Policy
All documents must be archived in secure digital repositories.
Contracts, documents and engineering data must be retained according to organizational and legal retention requirements.
Archived documents must remain accessible for audits, future reference and legal reference.
Records must be protected against unauthorized access or loss.
Deletion or disposal of records must require formal authorization.
Backup systems must ensure redundancy of data.
