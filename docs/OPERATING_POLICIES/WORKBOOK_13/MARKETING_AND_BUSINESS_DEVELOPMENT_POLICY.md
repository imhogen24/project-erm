13.1 Technical Marketing Content Feed
13.1 TECHNICAL MARKETING CONTENT FEED
13.1.1 Content Capture Protocol
Content Capture Protocol

All marketing content generated from a completed project must be tied to a valid Project Request ID before entry into the marketing hub.
Every content feed entry must document the client's original problem input alongside IMHOGEN's engineered solution output.
Content entries must include a quantifiable impact or benefit statement for each solution delivered.
Marketing content must accurately represent the materials, energy, and information transformation achieved by the project.
Content entries must not be submitted without verified technical accuracy from the responsible R&D Lead.

13.1.2 Process Story Documentation Policy
Process Story Documentation Policy

All project marketing content must include a documented process story covering the key QMS milestones visible to the client.
Each process milestone entry must include a plain-language description of what was done, a reference photo or render identifier, and a quality proof document reference.
Fabrication and testing stages must be supported by corresponding QMS document references such as in-process QC logs and function test reports.
The process story must present IMHOGEN's engineering methodology in terms accessible to non-technical clients.
No process milestone may be listed without at least one supporting quality evidence reference.

13.1.3 Channel Mapping and Call to Action Policy
Channel Mapping and Call to Action Policy

Every marketing content feed must specify the target distribution channels and the corresponding marketing objective for each channel.
Approved marketing channels include the company website, social media platforms, and professional conferences.
Each channel entry must include a specific link or message directive aligned with the marketing objective.
Conference and exhibition content must position IMHOGEN as a credible research and development organization.
No content may be distributed through a channel not listed and approved in the channel mapping section.

13.1.4 Content Approval and Transmittal Policy
Content Approval and Transmittal Policy

All technical marketing content must be certified by the R&D Lead as technically accurate before transmittal to the marketing hub.
Client confidentiality clearance must be explicitly confirmed by the R&D Lead prior to any content release.
The marketing hub must formally acknowledge receipt of all transmitted content feeds.
Content must not be released externally until both the R&D Lead signature and the marketing hub acknowledgment are recorded.
All approval dates must be recorded in the DD/MM/YYYY format.

13.2 Asset Marketing Brief
13.2 ASSET MARKETING BRIEF
13.2.1 Campaign Strategy Policy
Campaign Strategy Policy

Every marketing campaign initiated for a project deliverable must have a clearly defined campaign theme and key message.
The key message must communicate the transformation delivered to the client in technical yet accessible language.
Campaign strategy must be documented in the Asset Marketing Brief before any creative production begins.
Campaign themes must reflect IMHOGEN's engineering identity and align with the company's professional positioning.

13.2.2 Technical Content Accuracy Policy
Technical Content Accuracy Policy

All technical features included in marketing copy must be sourced from verified QMS documents such as needs assessments, metrology logs, or testing reports.
Each technical feature listed in the brief must be paired with a client-facing benefit statement explaining the practical value to the end user.
Source document references must be cited for every technical feature included in marketing materials.
Marketing copywriters must not alter or interpret technical specifications beyond what is documented in the source reference.
Technical content must be reviewed for accuracy before it is used in any public-facing marketing asset.

13.2.3 Call to Action Enforcement Policy
Call to Action Enforcement Policy

All marketing materials produced from this brief must include the mandatory company inquiry link directing users to the official product consultation form.
A clear and approved call-to-action button text must be specified for every digital marketing asset.
Every public post, email, and social media update must include a direct link back to the IMHOGEN inquiry form so that the QMS cycle is initiated for new project leads.
Marketing assets that do not include the mandatory CTA link must not be approved for distribution.

13.2.4 Hub Instructions and Brand Compliance
Hub Instructions and Brand Compliance

Marketing hub designers must apply the approved industrial color palette to all assets produced from this brief.
Visual content must emphasize the before-and-after transformation narrative when presenting project outcomes.
Social media posts must tag relevant institutional partners such as academic or industry collaborators where applicable.
All hub instructions specified in the brief must be implemented before the asset is submitted for final approval.

13.3 Visual Content Management
13.3 VISUAL CONTENT MANAGEMENT
13.3.1 Image Asset Curation Policy
Image Asset Curation Policy

All image assets used in marketing must be catalogued in the Visual Content Manifest before distribution.
Each image asset entry must include a unique asset name, asset type, file link or path, and a descriptive view description.
Image assets must include representative photographs of fabrication, testing, and final product output stages.
Three-dimensional renders used for marketing must clearly depict the product configuration delivered to the client.
Image assets must not be published without a valid file link or path confirmed in the manifest.

13.3.2 Video and Motion Asset Policy
Video and Motion Asset Policy

All video and motion assets used for marketing must be catalogued in the Visual Content Manifest with the asset name, duration, file link or path, and marketing purpose.
Video assets must demonstrate key performance outputs such as throughput tests and operational safety compliance.
Short-form video assets must be optimized for high-energy engagement on digital platforms.
Longer demonstration videos must document industrial safety compliance behaviors for credibility.
No video asset may be released without a confirmed file link and documented marketing purpose.

13.3.3 Brand Compliance Policy
Brand Compliance Policy

The IMHOGEN watermark must be applied to all visual assets before they are approved for public distribution.
All photographs and video content must be reviewed to ensure no visible safety violations are present before release.
Brand compliance checks must be documented in the Visual Content Manifest as part of the approval record.
Content containing safety violations must be withheld and submitted for review before any further action is taken.
All visual assets must meet IMHOGEN's visual identity standards prior to publication.

13.3.4 Authorization and Distribution Policy
Authorization and Distribution Policy

Visual assets must be formally cleared for public distribution by the R&D Lead before they are released to external channels.
The R&D Lead authorization signature and approval date must be recorded in the Visual Content Manifest for every release.
Assets that have not received formal authorization must not be shared, posted, or transmitted externally under any circumstances.
Authorization records must be maintained within the project documentation archive for audit and traceability purposes.

13.4 QMS Integration and Marketing Hub Governance
13.4 QMS INTEGRATION AND MARKETING HUB GOVERNANCE
13.4.1 QMS-Linked Marketing Policy
QMS-Linked Marketing Policy

Marketing content derived from project deliverables must reference the corresponding QMS documents that substantiate quality claims.
Quality evidence documents such as testing reports and in-process QC logs must be used as marketing proof material where applicable.
Marketing materials that reference technical performance must link claims to the verified QMS documentation from which they are drawn.
The marketing department must coordinate with the R&D division to ensure that all technical claims are supported by documented engineering evidence.
Marketing assets must not make performance claims that are not supported by corresponding project documentation.

13.4.2 Confidentiality and Data Governance Policy
Confidentiality and Data Governance Policy

Client identity, project specifics, and proprietary technical data must not be disclosed in marketing content without explicit client confidentiality clearance.
Clearance confirmation must be obtained and recorded in the content feed approval section before any project-specific marketing content is released.
All marketing content involving client project data must be treated as confidential until formal clearance is granted.
The R&D Lead is responsible for confirming that client confidentiality has been cleared prior to certification of any content for external release.
Marketing hub records containing client project data must be stored securely and accessed only by authorized personnel.
